Boarding & Welfare

The boarding department supports 1,429 students across 10 modern hostels, each managed by a dedicated hostel master.

  • The school provides a nutritious, balanced diet including various proteins and fresh fruits.
  • Health and safety are top priorities, supported by a 24/7 clinic and a qualified clinician.
  • Reliable referral systems are in place with Chuka General, Optimal Family, and Komarock hospitals.

Academic Excellence

Academic support is closely integrated into boarding life, where masters monitor student progress and provide guidance to ensure they remain focused.

The Languages Department is leading the transition from the 8-4-4 system to the Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC), emphasizing critical thinking, communication, and 21st-century values, with a full phase-out target of 2027.
